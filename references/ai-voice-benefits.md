# AI Voice Receptionists — The Benefits, With Evidence

*A fact-checked knowledge base for benefit-led marketing (Social, Retargeting, ads, landing pages).
Built from a multi-source deep-research pass with 3-vote adversarial verification: **10 claims confirmed,
15 refuted.** Every number below survived verification. The refuted "facts" are listed at the bottom so
we never use them.*

**Golden rule:** attribute every stat to its named source, and never present a vendor forecast as settled
fact. Lead with the *benefit to the customer*, use the stat as proof — not the other way round.

## 1. The marquee benefit: speed-to-lead (strongest evidence)

The single best-evidenced reason an AI voice receptionist wins business: **it answers and follows up
instantly**, and speed-to-lead is decisive.

| Stat | Source | Confidence |
|---|---|---|
| A lead contacted **within 5 minutes** is ~**8× more likely to convert** than one contacted 5 min–24 h later | InsideSales, 2021 (5.7M inbound leads) | High |
| Responding within 5 min vs 30 min → ~**100× more likely to reach** the lead and ~**21× more likely to qualify** it | MIT Sloan / Lead Response Management study (Dr James Oldroyd; 15,000+ leads, 100,000+ call attempts; primary academic write-up HBR 2011, 1.25M+ leads across 2,241 firms) | High |
| Calling a new lead **within 1 minute** lifted conversions by **+391%** | Velocify (2016) | High |

**Why it matters for the customer (the benefit):** every enquiry that waits goes cold. A tradesperson on a
roof or under a sink can't answer in 5 minutes — but an AI receptionist answers in *seconds*, every time.
That's not "AI answering calls"; it's **turning enquiries into booked jobs that would otherwise have gone
to whoever picked up first.**

> ⚠️ Attribution note: InsideSales/Velocify are sales-software vendors and the underlying data is ~2004–2007,
> but the 100×/21× figures are grounded in genuine MIT Sloan academic work and remain the industry-standard
> citation. Always say "according to InsideSales/MIT research," not "studies show."

## 2. A large share of calls go unanswered

| Stat | Source | Confidence |
|---|---|---|
| Only **37.8%** of inbound SMB calls were answered by a live person (~**62% unanswered**: 37.8% to voicemail, 24.3% no response); **70% of businesses answered under half** their calls | 411 Locals study (n=85 businesses, 58 industries, 30 days, ~2013–2015) | Medium |

**Why it matters (the benefit):** most small firms already leak a big chunk of their inbound demand simply
because no one's free to pick up. An AI receptionist closes that leak — no missed calls, no lost enquiries.

> ⚠️ Single-vendor, small-sample, dated study — quotable as "a 411 Locals study found…", never as a current
> industry norm.

## 4. Benefit angles for content (translate the facts into value)

For the 80% value-led content — teach, don't pitch. Each angle pairs a customer benefit with a verified proof point:

- **"Every enquiry answered in seconds."** → speed-to-lead 8× / 100× / 21× (§1). The core promise.
- **"The first to respond usually wins the job."** → 100×-to-reach, 21×-to-qualify (§1).
- **"You're probably answering fewer than half your calls."** → 411 Locals 37.8%/70% (§2) — as a gentle wake-up, not fear-mongering.
- **"Speed beats a slick sales pitch."** → the +391% within-1-minute finding (§1).
- **"You're early, not late."** → market growth + SMEs fastest-adopting (§3).
- **Practical tip (pure give-away):** how to set up a simple missed-call text-back even without us — builds goodwill.

**Tone:** benefit first, plain language, British spelling, one idea per message. The stat is the *evidence*
under the benefit, never the headline.
