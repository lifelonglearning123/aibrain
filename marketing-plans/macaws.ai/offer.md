## 1. The current offer, stated plainly

Pulled from what the brain has learned you actually say and sell:

- **Name:** none — you sell "an AI voice bot" / "AI automation." (This alone costs you: unnamed services compete on price.)
- **Core:** an AI voice bot — inbound receptionist and/or outbound lead-caller — connected to the client's CRM, calling leads within seconds, sending recording + transcript + summary. Often white-labelled on a subdomain. Sometimes expanded to SMS/email/voice sequences and database reactivation.
- **Price:** setup fee + monthly retainer (⚠️ "low-to-mid hundreds" £/mo), commission splits with lead-supplying partners.
- **Guarantee:** none.
- **Scarcity:** none.
- **Bonuses:** none formalised.

**Diagnosis:** five of the six offer components are missing. You have a strong *core* and a vague *price*, and nothing else. That's not a copy problem — it's an offer problem.

## 2. Value-Equation scorecard

```
          Dream Outcome  ×  Perceived Likelihood
Value  =  ─────────────────────────────────────────
          Time Delay     ×   Effort & Sacrifice
```

| Lever | Score /10 | Why (from your VOC) |
|---|---|---|
| **Dream outcome** | 5 | The outcome ("never miss a lead again → more booked jobs") is excellent, but you sell the *mechanism* ("AI voice bot"), not the outcome. Latent, not articulated. |
| **Perceived likelihood** | **3 — binding constraint** | New category. Your own objections are all belief/risk: "is this real?", CRM feels buggy, "who handles booked appointments?", transparency worries, can't-promise-outcomes on thin data. Buyers don't yet believe it'll work for *them*. |
| **Time delay** | 5 | The bot is instant *once live* — but time-to-live is slow: payment/DD setup delays and multi-week onboarding are top momentum-killers in your data. |
| **Effort & sacrifice** | 6 | Done-for-you is a real strength; dragged down by onboarding friction (CRM access, scripts, compliance registration, lead upload). |

**The binding constraint is Perceived Likelihood.** The offer must be engineered to make a skeptical trades owner *believe it's real and low-risk* — that's the guarantee's job. Second priority: crush Time Delay (speed to live).

## 4. The designed offer — **"The AI Front Desk"**

**One-line promise:**
> *Every call answered and every new lead called back within 60 seconds — under your brand — with the recording, transcript and summary in your inbox. Live in 5 working days.*

That names the dream outcome (you never miss a lead again) and front-loads the proof mechanism.

### Core deliverable
- **AI voice receptionist (inbound)** + **AI lead-caller (outbound)**, trained on *your* qualifying questions and best appointment-setter's script.
- **Connected to your CRM/GHL** — calls every new lead within 60 seconds.
- **Every call → recording + transcript + summary** delivered to you (this artefact *is* the proof; make it Day 1).
- **Branded as you** — your name, your voice, on your subdomain. The bot is transparent that it's an assistant (never pretends to be human — your own rule, and it builds trust).
- **Business-hours support** included; 24/7 as a paid upgrade (matches your VOC caution — don't over-promise cover you can't staff).

### Bonus stack (additive and honest — each compounds the core)
1. **"Lead Rescue" reactivation campaign (month 1)** — the bot works your existing dormant lead list on day one, so ROI can show up *before* new leads even arrive. Uses an asset they already own; high perceived value.
2. **Done-for-you compliance setup** — business registration for outbound calling/SMS handled for them. A real friction from your data, turned into a gift.
3. **Script & qualifying-question build** — we build the call flow from your best setter's script, not a generic template.
4. **Monthly performance recap** — "here's what your Front Desk did this month" (calls handled, leads qualified, appointments booked). Doubles as your retention + upsell hook.

*No inflated "£X value" tags — specificity beats inflation, and trades buyers smell padding.*

### Guarantee — the lever (two parts, both honest and deliverable)

Your buyer's two real fears are *"what if it doesn't work / isn't real?"* and *"what if you take forever?"* So:

> **1. The 5-Day Go-Live promise.** Your Front Desk is live within 5 working days of us getting access — or you don't pay the setup fee.
>
> **2. The first-month pilot gate.** Month 1 is a paid pilot. If your Front Desk isn't reliably answering calls and calling new leads back within 60 seconds — with the recording, transcript and summary delivered — you don't pay for month 2, and we part on good terms.

**Why this exact design (and not the obvious one):** the tempting guarantee is "we'll book you X appointments or your money back." **Don't** — bookings depend on lead quality you can't control, and your own data warns against promising outcomes on thin data. That's an *outcome* guarantee that explodes publicly when it fails. Instead this is a **service-level + process-performance** guarantee: you promise the machine *does its job* (speed + delivery of artefacts), which is fully in your control and measurable. It reverses the buyer's risk without betting your reputation on their lead list.

*Stress-test (per the skill): if 10% invoke it, you refund a setup fee / lose a month-2 — survivable, and you keep the goodwill. An outcome guarantee at 10% invoke would be ruinous.*

### Scarcity — real, not fake
> *We take on a limited number of new Front Desk setups each month so every go-live gets full attention. Next slots open [date].*

This is genuinely true — done-for-you delivery is capacity-constrained (⚠️ set the real monthly number). **No countdown timers, no "3 spots left" theatre** — that torches trust with exactly this kind of skeptical buyer.

### Price + payment (with the friction fix baked in)
- **Setup fee + monthly retainer**, tiered by the client's existing ad spend / expected value (your own pricing logic). ⚠️ Confirm exact numbers — needed for the plan's CAC/LTV math.
- **Suggested tiers (⚠️ placeholder):** Starter (inbound receptionist) → Growth (+ outbound lead-caller & follow-up) → Scale (+ multi-channel sequences & reactivation). Clear value step-ups make upgrades obvious.
- **Instant payment link to start while direct debit is arranged** — straight from your own winning play. This removes your #1 momentum-killer *inside the offer*, not as an afterthought.

## 5. Why this works — mapped back to the levers

- **Perceived likelihood ↑↑** (the binding constraint): the pilot gate + 5-day SLA + real recording/transcript proof give a skeptical buyer *permission to try* at near-zero risk.
- **Time delay ↓**: 5-day go-live SLA + payment link kill the activation lag.
- **Effort & sacrifice ↓**: done-for-you + compliance-as-a-bonus removes the onboarding load.
- **Dream outcome ↑**: naming it "The AI Front Desk / never miss a lead" sells the result, not the tech.

All four levers move, and the weakest one moves most. That's a correctly-sequenced offer.

## 6. The second offer — **White-Label Partner Program** (your highest-margin channel)

Different buyer (agency/reseller, sophisticated B2B), so a different offer — do **not** bolt the consumer guarantee onto it.

- **Core:** licence to resell the AI Front Desk under *their* brand — clone the demo, rebrand, deploy on their subdomain, connect their client's CRM. (You already do this; productise it.)
- **Bonuses:** the clone-rebrand-deploy kit, a partner playbook, and co-selling support on their first deal.
- **Terms not guarantee:** transparent margin / commission split ("long-term, not transactional" — your words). Sophisticated buyers want commercial terms, not refunds.
- **Scarcity:** a limited number of partners per region so you're not arming competitors in the same patch.
- **Price:** licence + margin, or commission split on partner-supplied leads.

## 7. Honest lift projection

Adding a name + guarantee + real scarcity + de-risked pilot to an offer that has **none** typically lifts conversion 10–40% *per component*, with the guarantee/pilot being the big one for a new-category, skeptical buyer. Two levers stacked (risk-reversal + speed-to-live) can compound. **No 5× promises** — anyone selling that is selling something. The real unlock is simpler and bigger: trades owners currently have no low-risk way to say yes, and this gives them one.
