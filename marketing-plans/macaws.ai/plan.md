## 1. Executive summary

**The one-line read:** macaws.ai is not an awareness problem. It's a **conversion-and-recurring-revenue** problem. The brain shows a large open pipeline but effectively £0 recurring revenue flowing (⚠️ partly a TEST-mode Stripe artefact — confirm). Pouring more leads into a funnel that isn't converting to *paying, retained* subscribers would waste money. The 12 months are about turning demand you already have into monthly recurring revenue, then systematising the machine that does it.

**Three big bets:**

1. **Productise one wedge offer and make it convert.** Lead with the AI voice bot ("every lead called within seconds, qualified like your best setter, with recording + transcript + summary") as the *land* offer, with a hard guarantee and a fixed setup→live timeline. Your own VOC says the voice bot "has higher market value and can anchor the wider automation offering." Stop selling a platform; sell one outcome.

2. **Fix activation: demo → live in days, not weeks.** Payment/DD setup and unclear appointment-ownership are killing momentum (both are top objections in your data). Build a repeatable onboarding runway (clone demo → rebrand → subdomain → CRM connect → go live) with a payment link that unblocks the direct-debit wait.

3. **Turn delivery into a compounding engine.** White-label + reseller is your highest-margin path. Package a "resell macaws under your brand" motion and a retention/expansion ladder (voice bot → multi-channel sequences → database reactivation) so each client's ARPA climbs.

**90-day priority:** one packaged offer, one guarantee, one onboarding runway, and the brain's own macaws Business Context profile filled in (it's currently empty — the foundation everything else reads from).

**12-month outcome (target, ⚠️ pending baseline):** a predictable land-and-expand motion — a defined wedge offer, a sub-1-week activation time, a reseller channel live, and recurring revenue that actually registers in Stripe.

## 2. Strategic frame

**Category claim:** "Your AI front desk and follow-up team — answering, qualifying and chasing every lead within seconds, under your brand." Not "an AI sales & marketing SaaS platform" (too broad, sells features). The narrower the wedge, the faster it converts.

**ICP (distilled from real data):**
- **Primary — SMB / trades-services owner-operators** personally handling their own sales and admin, losing leads to slow response and missed calls. High pain, fast decision, needs done-for-you.
- **Secondary — agencies, coaches and lead-gen resellers** with paid-ad funnels who need instant speed-to-lead and want a white-label AI product to resell to *their* clients. Higher ACV, channel leverage.
- **Anti-persona:** enterprises wanting deep custom builds and 24/7 SLAs before revenue exists — your data explicitly warns against over-promising 24/7 support and open-ended custom work.

**Business-model logic:** monthly retainers (⚠️ "low-to-mid hundreds" £/mo per the data) + setup fees + commission splits with lead-supplying partners + white-label reseller margin. Recurring is the prize; setup fees fund delivery; reseller multiplies reach without headcount.

**Brand voice non-negotiables (from real winning phrases):** plain, concrete, operator-to-operator ("Let's package the offer, upload the leads, and test SMS, email and voice to see who books"). Show the mechanism, not hype. British English. **Transparency rule from your own objections data: never imply the bot is human** — it's a named strength, not a thing to hide.

## 4. Acquisition (strangers → aware)

Do **less** here than instinct says until activation converts. Focus acquisition on the two channels your VOC already validates.

**Channels — keep / add / skip:**
- **Keep: warm outbound + partner/reseller sourcing.** Your winning motions are 1:1 ("clone the demo, rebrand, deploy as a subdomain"). Systematise it (skill: `prospecting` → `cold-email`), targeting trades owners and agencies with ad funnels.
- **Add (organic proof): short demo content.** You literally have recordings, transcripts and "previous-work videos" mentioned in the data. Turn real bot calls into proof-driven posts and short video (this is where the Brain's Social + the new video pipeline feed in). Skill: `social`, `content-strategy`.
- **Add (owned demand): a single sharp landing page + book-a-demo.** Mirror the ad/outbound headline to the page (skill: `cro`, `copywriting`).
- **Skip for now: broad paid social / SEO content marathons.** No budget confirmed and the funnel isn't ready. Local search ads are a *client* tactic you sell — not necessarily your own top priority yet.

**90-day moves:** (1) one landing page with the wedge offer + demo booking; (2) a 15-touch outbound sequence to trades + agencies grounded in the real pain points; (3) a weekly proof post/video from real bot calls.
**12-month moves:** reseller-led acquisition (partners bring their own clients), local-SEO/GBP once the offer converts, AI-SEO for "best AI receptionist for UK trades" (skill: `ai-seo`).

## 5. Activation (aware → first valued experience) — **the priority stage**

This is where the money is being lost. Two objections in your data are activation-killers: **payment/DD setup delays** and **unclear appointment ownership**.

**Moves:**
- **Onboarding runway (productised):** clone demo → rebrand → subdomain → connect CRM/API token → upload leads → test SMS/email/voice → go live. Turn this into a checklist with an owner and a target: **demo → live in ≤5 working days.** Skill: `onboarding`.
- **Kill the payment delay:** offer an instant payment link to start while direct debit is arranged (straight from your own winning play). Removes the #1 momentum-killer.
- **Resolve appointment ownership up front:** a one-question qualifier at sale — "who handles booked appointments: you, us, or a partner?" — baked into the sales script and onboarding. Skill: `revops`.
- **First-value moment:** the client's first real lead called within seconds, with recording + transcript + summary delivered. That artefact *is* the aha. Make delivering it Day 1, not Week 2.

**Landing/CRO:** audit the demo-booking page for the classic leaks — value-prop clarity, one CTA, trust (real call recordings as proof), objection handling (compliance, transparency, support hours). Skill: `cro`.

## 6. Retention (converted → stays and deepens)

You have zero lifecycle automation and GHL (email/SMS) sitting wired and idle — this is free money.

**Moves:**
- **Onboarding email/SMS sequence:** setup steps, what's needed (CRM access, scripts, qualification questions — all in your FAQ data), first-week check-in. Skill: `emails`.
- **Performance-proof loop:** monthly "here's what your bot did" recap (calls handled, leads qualified, appointments booked) — retention *and* upsell fuel.

- **Failed-payment recovery (dunning):** the moment Stripe is live for real. Skill: `emails`/`churn-prevention`.

## 8. Revenue (monetisation)

> **Full offer design in [`offer.md`](offer.md)** — built with the `offers` skill. Summary below.

**The binding constraint is belief, not price.** The value-equation scorecard puts *perceived likelihood of achievement* at 3/10 — every top objection in your data ("is this real?", CRM feels buggy, "who handles bookings?", transparency) is a risk/belief issue. So the offer is engineered to make a skeptical trades owner believe it's real and low-risk.

**The land offer — "The AI Front Desk":**
- **Promise:** every call answered and every lead called back within 60 seconds, under your brand, with recording + transcript + summary — live in 5 working days.
- **Guarantee (the lever):** *(1)* 5-day go-live or you don't pay setup; *(2)* month 1 is a paid pilot — if the bot isn't reliably hitting the 60-second speed + delivering the artefacts, you don't pay for month 2. **Deliberately a service-level + process guarantee, NOT an outcome/bookings guarantee** — bookings depend on lead quality you can't control, and your own data warns against promising outcomes on thin data.
- **Bonus stack:** "Lead Rescue" reactivation in month 1 (ROI before new leads arrive) · done-for-you compliance setup · script build from your best setter · monthly performance recap.
- **Scarcity:** real, capacity-based (N setups/month) — no fake countdowns.
- **Payment:** instant payment link to start while direct debit is arranged — kills your #1 momentum-killer inside the offer.
- **Expand ladder:** receptionist → outbound lead-caller → multi-channel sequences → database reactivation. Each step raises ARPA.

**The channel offer — White-Label Partner Program:** resell the Front Desk under the partner's brand; transparent margin/commission (terms, not a guarantee — sophisticated buyers). Your highest-margin path.

**Pricing:** tier by the client's ad spend / expected value (your logic). ⚠️ Exact setup + retainer numbers are an open decision — needed for CAC/LTV math.

**Fix the measurement first:** the TEST-mode Stripe key means revenue is invisible to the brain. Until that's live, every revenue number is guesswork. A §13 open decision, but really a Week-1 task.
